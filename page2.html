Introduction of Artist

I am Gontse J. Sithole, and I come from a small suburban area called Lotus Gardens in the city of Pretoria. I attended Holy Trinity Catholic Secondary School in Atteridgeville, and after completing high school, I went to study a BA in Fine Arts at the University of the Witwatersrand in Johannesburg. I completed my degree and was awarded the Anya Millman Scholarship for outstanding practical work in 2021.

My dream of becoming an artist started when I was in primary school. I was always fascinated by drawing, but when I transitioned to secondary school, I found my skill in building structures and photographing them. I am one person who has always seen the world differently, always asking myself questions such as why things are the way they are and not in a different form. This led me to the concept of change, the process at which things or an object takes to change.

I aim to question all the stages or rather the process of one thing changing into another, and how that can automatically twist the meaning or add to the meaning of that particular object or idea. 

"Change is an automatic continuation from one end to another and it can never be stopped."

Gontse Sithole.

What Drives Me To Create?

As an artist, I'm driven by a relentless curiosity that has been with me since childhood. I remember being captivated by the simple act of drawing, and later, the thrill of constructing and capturing structures through photography. This innate desire to explore and understand the world has led me to a deep fascination with change, the transformative journey from one state to another. I'm drawn to the power of art to challenge perceptions, spark conversations, and ultimately, inspire others to see the world in a new light.
